Quo Vadis, Kit?

Why this city? How is it? My checklist

Why should you care?

{ Photo of the Old Town Square by Raul Cacho Oses }
  1. Founded as early as the 9th century
  2. Packed with historical buildings spanning across centuries
  3. Capital of Czech Republic

How is it?

{ Photo of Matilda - the boat, by Dominik Dvořák }
  • Prague is full of tourist traps, usually in the form of irrelevant food or a bad coffee, best to follow the local hipsters and white-collar workers when in the centre
  • The riverways that have been inspiring artists for centuries are accessible, and you can rent a paddleboat to explore them in the city centre
  • Attending an opera or ballet performance in the State Opera, National or Estate Theatre is comparably cheap to the world and seeing the beautiful interiors itself worth the investment

What to do in Prague?

{ Photo of the city centre by JESHOOTS.COM }
  • Dress up smart to visit nearly daily performances in one of the many theatres
  • Board the cable car to Petrin to visit the tower's lookout and the mirror maze
  • Explore Czech art in DOX Centre for Contemporary Art, and visit its airship
  • Wander through Josefov, the birthplace of Kafka, with its museums and Prague's Jewish heritage - can you find both statues of Kafka?
  • Take on a challenge to visit all seven historic buildings (palaces) of the National Gallery in Prague, possibly after purchasing the Club of Friends membership card for unlimited entries to all exhibitions
  • Promenade along the Vltava river with museums and gallery spaces nearby
  • Cross the famous Charles Bridge in early morning or night to avoid crowds
  • Dine alfresco on riverboats near Manes or Cech's bridge
  • Follow local hipsters to the good coffee places in Karlin or Dejvice
  • Look for animals in the Wallenstein Palace Gardens dripstone wall
  • See the astronomical clock to strike and do its thing, best at night
